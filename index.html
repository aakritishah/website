Bio

Hi! I am a Computer Science Master's student at USC with a Bachelor's in Computer Science and Business Management. My research focuses on machine learning for social good, with interests in Natural Language Processing (LLMs), Computer Vision, and Healthcare AI. My research experience lies in unlearning in LLMs, digital pathology cancer detection, deep learning, computer vision, and adversarial ML.

Research

  • Digital Pathology Prostate Cancer (AIMed Lab): Prostate cancer detection via tissue compartment segmentation (UNet, nnUNet, UNI). Current Dice score is ~0.845.
    Introducing a method to reincorporate lumen in a postprocessing step and evaluating deep learning vs. foundational segmentation approaches.
    Abstract Project

  • Unlearning in LLMs (ARKAI Lab): Studying targeted unlearning in LLMs (Llama-2-7b) using Harry Potterâ€“unlearned models, applying ACT theory from psychology.
    Exploring how forgetting in LLMs parallels entanglement of human memory, testing adversarial and persuasive prompting techniques.
    Project

  • Diabetic Retinopathy Detection: Diabetic Retinopathy detection with preprocessing (CLAHE, Gaussian Blur, MGA-CSG) to improve retinal fundus image quality.
    Focused on combating poor dataset quality by optimizing preprocessing for early detection and improving model robustness.

  • Conversational Hinglish Chatbot: Built with multilingual and synthetic datasets, fine-tuned LLMs for open-domain dialogue.
    Curated and augmented code-switched data, applied instruction-tuning, and evaluated model outputs on syntactic and semantic quality.
    Paper Project

  • Evaluating AI Sentiment Analysis: Compared human vs. LLM sentiment analysis on nonprofit interview data in nuanced contexts.
    Manually annotated interview data and explored implications of LLM sentiment performance in low-resource and emotionally nuanced domains.
    Paper Project

  • Adversarial Attacks in Autonomous Driving Systems: Investigated adversarial robustness in ML for autonomous driving.
    Analyzed transfer learning and robustness across model architectures; participated in weekly cross-disciplinary research ethics seminars.
